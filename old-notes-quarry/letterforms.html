Letter forms

alpha

alpha
see beginning of word αὐτοὺς in Iliad 1.3

beta

beta
one of the most different-loooking letter forms. See beginning of word βουλή in Iliad 1.5. Contrast with this the much taller first stroke of kappa

gamma

gamma
gamma is happy to slide into a following letter, as in ὁ γὰρ in Iliad 1.9

epsilon

epsilon
easy to recognize by itself, as in the δὲ in the phrase ὀλέκοντο δὲ λαοὶ in Iliad 1.10 , but combines in complex ligatures more readily than any other letter. (Be sure to see the page of ligatures!)

eta
similar shape to kappa with a long downward stroke, but eta returns to a higher and sharper point. ἡρώων in Iliad 1.4

iota

iota
Classical iota subscript written adscript: θυμῶι in Iliad 1.24

kappa

kappa
distinguished from the cursive beta by its taller starting point, see the kappa in the middle of ὀλέκοντο in the phrase ὀλέκοντο δὲ λαοὶ in Iliad 1.10

lambda

lambda
the first part of the stroke sinks well below the line, normally at a steep diagonal, and the second part curves down to the base line, sometimes almost like a C. Compare the lambdas in the phrase ὀλέκοντο δὲ λαοὶ in Iliad 1.10 .

mu

mu
the first downward stroke falls well below the line; in contrast to nu, the second downward stroke regularly comes back to the baseline. See the first letter of μυρί’ in Iliad 1.2

nu

nu
looks a little like a modern mu, but note that while the first downward stroke normally comes down very low, there is no second downward stroke like that of mu. See the final nu of ἡρώων in Iliad 1.4

pi

pi
like modern Greek cursive form, the bottom section is written in one motion, so that the two legs of the pi become a pair of loops, like the first letter of προΐαψεν in Iliad 1.3

rho

rho
readily combines with preceding letter, normally a clear break between rho and following letter, see ἡρώων in Iliad 1.4

upsilon
when unconnected to following letter, the form is familiar as in οὐλομένην in Iliad 1.2, but see following example.

upsilon
when joined to a following letter, the upward stroke of the upsilon does not necessarily come much above the line, as in αὐτοὺς in Iliad 1.3

psi

psi
very flat, almost like a crossed T, see προΐαψεν in Iliad 1.3

omega

omega
normally has a closed, "hour-glass" form, as in the two omegas of ἡρώων in Iliad 1.4
